ABOUT


Aim: Performing short circuit test on single phase transformer to calculate:


    1.Copper losses of the transformer
    2.Circuit parameters of the transformer [Resistance, Reactance and Impedence].


Apparatus:

Theory:


 1. A single-phase 1KVA, 230V/115V step-down transformer as shown in Fig(1) is used to determine the equivalent circuit parameters and copper losses
  of the transformer. It operates on the principle of mutual induction.

 2. The primary side is the high-voltage side, consisting of 0-230V, and the secondary side is the low-voltage side, consisting of 0-115V.

 3. This transformer converts high-voltage, low-current power into low-voltage, high-current power.

 4. It is used to step down the voltage from 230V to 115V.

Procedure:



 1. Switch 'ON' the relay to power on the device.

 2. Move the slider from 0V to 5.6V and wait.

 3. Note down the values for voltage (Vsc), current (Isc), and power (Wsc) from Table-1 [Observations]. Consider the power (Wsc) value as copper losses.

 4. Calculate the circuit parameters (Resistance, Reactance, and Impedance) on both the Low Voltage side and High Voltage side using the given formulas below.

 5. To repeat the experiment, use the "Recalibrate" button to perform the experiment again.
