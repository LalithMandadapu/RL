ABOUT



Aim: Performing short circuit test on single phase transformer to calculate:


    1.Copper losses of the transformer.
    2.Circuit parameters of the transformer [Resistance, Reactance and Impedence].


Apparatus Required:

S.No Name of the Component Rating
1 Transformer 1KVA
2 Auto-transformer 230V/0-270V
3 Voltmeter 0-30V
4 Ammeter 0-5A
5 Watt Meter 0-75W


Theory:


 1.  In this test, the low voltage winding is usually short circuited by thick wire and supply is given to the high voltage winding.

 2.  The supply to the high voltage winding is given by using auto-transformer.

 3.  The applied voltage Vsc is gradually increases till the ammeter shows the full load current.

 4.  As the applied voltage Vsc is very low ( only 5 – 10% of the normal voltage ), core flux is very small, iron losses are small
    so it is neglected.

 5.  The wattmeter Wsc shows full load copper losses of the transformer.

 6. Full load copper losses of both winding Wsc = Isc² R01


    ⸫ R01 = Wsc/Isc²

 7.  Equivalent impedance as referred to primary side:

    Zsc = Z01 = Vsc/ Isc


    ⸫ X01 = √ ( Z01² – R01²)


 8.  The voltage transformation ratio ( k ) can easily find out by taking reading of HV winding and LV winding.


 9.  The transformer equivalent circuit parameter can easily determined as referred to secondary side after calculating voltage
   transformation ratio (k).

   R02 = R01/k²


   X02 = X01/k²


   Z02 = Z01/k²
