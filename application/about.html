ABOUT



Aim: To perform short circuit test on single phase transformer to calculate:


  1. Copper losses of the transformer.
  2. Circuit parameters of the transformer [Resistance, Reactance and Impedence].

Apparatus Required:


S.No Name of the Component Rating
1 Transformer 1KVA
2 Auto-transformer 230V/0-270V
3 Voltmeter 0-30V
4 Ammeter 0-5A
5 Watt Meter 0-75W


Theory:


  1. In this test, the low voltage winding is usually short-circuited by thick wire, and supply is given to the high voltage winding.
  2. The supply to the high voltage winding is given by using an auto-transformer.
  3. The applied voltage Vsc gradually increases until the ammeter shows the full load current.
  4. As the applied voltage Vsc is very low (only 5 – 10% of the normal voltage), core flux is very small, and iron losses are negligible.
  5. The wattmeter Wsc shows the full load copper losses of the transformer.
  6. Full load copper losses of both windings Wsc = Isc² R01.

    7. Therefore, R01 = Wsc/Isc²

  7. Equivalent impedance referred to the primary side:

    8. Zsc = Z01 = Vsc/Isc

    Therefore, X01 = √(Z01² – R01²)

  8. The voltage transformation ratio (k) can be easily found by taking readings of both HV winding and LV winding.
  9. The transformer equivalent circuit parameters can be easily determined, referred to the secondary side, after calculating the voltage transformation ratio (k).

    10. R02 = R01/k²

    X02 = X01/k²

    Z02 = Z01/k²

Procedure:


  1. Switch 'ON' the relay to power on the device.
  2. Move the slider from 0V to 5.6V and wait.
  3. Note down the values for voltage (Vsc), current (Isc), and power (Wsc) from Table-1 [Observations]. Consider the power (Wsc) value as copper losses.
  4. Calculate the circuit parameters (Resistance, Reactance, and Impedance) on both the Low Voltage side and High Voltage side using the given formulas below.
  5. To repeat the experiment, use the "Recalibrate" button to perform the experiment again.

Calculations:


[Wsc = 35W, Isc = 4.35 (from Table-1)]

High voltage side Resistance R02 = Wsc / Isc²

Wsc = Isc² * R02

High volatage side Impedance Z02 = Vsc /Isc

High voltage side Reactance X02 = √ Z02²-R02²

K = 230V /115V = 2 [ Transformer Ratio]

Low voltage side Resistance R01 = R02 / K²

Low voltage side Reactance X01 = X02 / K²

Low voltage side Impedance Z01 = √ R01²+ X01²


Example:


High voltage side Resistance R02 = Wsc / Isc² = 35/(4.35)² = 1.84 Ω

Wsc = Isc² * R02 = (4.35)² * 1.84 = 10.25 Ω

High volatage side Impedance Z02 = Vsc /Isc = 8.05/4.35 = 1.85 Ω

High voltage side Reactance X02 = √ Z02²-R02² = √ (1.85)²-(1.84)² = 0.04 Ω

K = 230V /115V = 2 [ Transformer Ratio]

Low voltage side Resistance R01 = R02 / K² = 1.84/2² = 0.46 Ω

Low voltage side Reactance X01 = X02 / K² = 0.04/2² = 0.01 Ω

Low voltage side Impedance Z01 = √ R01²+ X01² = √ (0.46)²+ (0.01)² = 0.46 Ω



Conclusion:


  1. The wattmeter shows full load copper losses and iron losses of the transformer.
  2. As the supply voltage is only small amount of rated voltage, iron losses is neglected thus the wattmeter shows full load copper losses.
